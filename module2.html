Module Overview

This intermediate module builds on foundational skills to explore relationships between variables using bivariate and multivariate analysis techniques in R. You'll learn to conduct, interpret, and present sophisticated statistical analyses commonly used in health research.

Prerequisite: Completion of Module 1 or equivalent knowledge

Price: 20,000 FCFA ($25)

Duration: 4 weeks (2 lessons per week + live sessions)

Register for Module 2

Week 1: Bivariate Analysis 1

Explore fundamental techniques for analyzing relationships between two variables, focusing on proportions and quantitative variables.

Lesson 1: Comparison of Proportions and Correlation Between Two Quantitative Variables (Theory)

  • Chi-square tests for categorical data
  • Fisher's exact test for small samples
  • Pearson and Spearman correlation coefficients
  • Assumptions and interpretation of results

Lesson 2: Comparison of Proportions and Correlation Between Two Quantitative Variables (Practice in R)

  • Implementing chi-square tests in R
  • Calculating correlation coefficients
  • Visualizing bivariate relationships
  • Interpreting R output for bivariate analyses

Live Session 1: Week 1 Recap & Discussion

  • Q&A on bivariate analysis concepts
  • Case study analysis
  • Practical problem-solving exercises

Week 2: Bivariate Analysis 2

Extend bivariate analysis techniques to compare means across groups.

Lesson 3: Comparison of Means (Theory)

  • t-tests (independent and paired samples)
  • ANOVA for multiple group comparisons
  • Non-parametric alternatives (Mann-Whitney, Wilcoxon, Kruskal-Wallis)
  • Assumptions and post-hoc testing

Lesson 4: Comparison of Means (Practice in R)

  • Implementing t-tests and ANOVA in R
  • Checking assumptions (normality, homogeneity of variance)
  • Post-hoc analysis and multiple comparisons
  • Visualizing group differences

Live Session 2: Week 2 Recap & Discussion

  • Interpreting ANOVA results
  • Handling violations of assumptions
  • Hands-on analysis exercises

Week 3: Multivariate Analysis 1

Transition from bivariate to multivariate analysis with regression techniques.

Lesson 5: Interpretation of Bivariate Analysis Results

  • Common pitfalls in bivariate analysis
  • Introduction to confounding
  • Effect modification vs. confounding
  • When to move to multivariate analysis

Lesson 6: Linear and Logistic Regressions (Theory)

  • Linear regression for continuous outcomes
  • Logistic regression for binary outcomes
  • Model assumptions and diagnostics
  • Interpreting coefficients and odds ratios

Live Session 3: Week 3 Recap & Discussion

  • Case studies in regression analysis
  • Model building strategies
  • Q&A on regression concepts

Week 4: Multivariate Analysis 2

Implement and interpret regression models in R with practical applications.

Lesson 7: Linear and Logistic Regressions (Practice in R)

  • Fitting regression models in R
  • Model diagnostics and validation
  • Variable selection techniques
  • Presenting regression results

Lesson 8: Interpretation of Multivariate Analysis Results

  • Translating statistical output to meaningful conclusions
  • Assessing model fit and performance
  • Communicating results to non-technical audiences
  • Common mistakes in interpretation

Live Session 4: Week 4 Recap & Discussion

  • Comprehensive case study analysis
  • Final Q&A session
  • Module summary and next steps
  • Certificate requirements
